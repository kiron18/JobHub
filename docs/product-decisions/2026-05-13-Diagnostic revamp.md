The content here—the actual advice in that expanded screenshot—is brilliant, highly specific, and genuinely valuable. It is exactly the kind of high-level analysis an international grad needs.

However, the packaging of this advice currently violates the "Calm Ally" philosophy we established. Right now, this page feels like a high-pressure intervention rather than a strategic briefing. It triggers the exact anxieties we are trying to soothe.

Let’s break down why it feels overwhelming and how to apply the "Calm Ally" filter to this Diagnostic Report.

1. The FOMO Marketing (The Biggest Red Flag)
Look at the text right above the main call-to-action (CTA):

"Every day you delay is another day Australians with connections get the jobs you want."

The Problem: This is pure, weaponized anxiety. You are taking the exact deepest, darkest fear of an international graduate (that the system is rigged for locals) and using it as a high-pressure sales tactic. A "Calm Ally" never uses fear to force a conversion. It shatters the trust you just built with the good advice above it.
The Fix: Shift from FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) to Agency.

Change to: "You have the experience. Now let's build the narrative. Turn this diagnosis into an interview-ready resume."

2. The Headline & Framing
"Hey Kiron, here's what's holding back your Marketing job search."

The Problem: It starts the entire experience on a negative, heavy note. It implies personal failure ("holding you back"). The neon gradient on "Marketing" also feels a bit "SaaS-bro" rather than a premium consulting experience.
The Fix: Frame it as a strategic gap analysis, not a personal failing.

Change to: "Your Marketing Job Search: Strategic Diagnosis."

Sub-text: "We analyzed your application profile. Here is the gap between your current resume and the Lead-level roles you are targeting."

3. The Visual "Warning" Signs
The Problem: Look at the dots next to the section titles. "Document Audit" and "The Honest Truth" have Red dots. "Targeting" has an Orange dot. In a grief-shaped context, red means danger, error, or failure. It spikes cortisol before they even read the words.
The Fix: Move to the muted "Greenfield" palette we discussed earlier. Use neutral tones (like slate or muted gold) to indicate a section has actionable advice. Do not color-code by "severity."

4. Naming Conventions ("The Honest Truth")
The Problem: Naming a section "The Honest Truth" implies that a harsh scolding is coming, or that the rest of the report wasn't the truth. It causes the user to brace for impact.
The Fix: Keep the titles objective and professional. Instead of "The Honest Truth," use "The Core Blocker" or "Primary Strategic Gap."

5. Formatting the "Wall of Text" (The Expanded View)
The advice in the expanded "Targeting" tab is fantastic, but it is visually exhausting. When a user is anxious, their reading comprehension drops. Dense paragraphs look like a mountain to climb.

The Fix: Act like a Consultant. Break the dense text into scannable, comparative blocks.

Instead of a 4-sentence paragraph explaining the mismatch, format it like this:

The Targeting Gap
You are aiming for Lead-level Finance roles, but your resume tells a different story.

What Lead Finance Roles Require:

Brand strategy and regulatory-aware campaigns.

Stakeholder management across product/compliance teams.

Strategic leadership over a team.

What Your Resume Currently Shows:

Tactical execution (ad optimization, content creation).

Focus on events, education, and crypto (no finance language).

Most recent title is Coordinator in Toowoomba, not Sydney.

Then, follow up with the "Reframe your target or reframe your resume" section, which is excellent and highly actionable.

Summary: The Diagnosis of the Diagnosis
The AI is doing its job perfectly—the insights are sharp. The UI just needs to stop acting like an alarm bell and start acting like a blueprint.

Remove the high-pressure, fear-based CTA.

Remove the red/orange "error" colors.

Break dense paragraphs into scannable bullet points.

Change the tone from "Here is why you are failing" to "Here is the gap we need to close."

If you make these tweaks, this report will transform from a source of dread into a massive sigh of relief for the user. They finally have a clear map.

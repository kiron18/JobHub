THE ANALYSIS MAPPING conundrum :If the AI only looks at existing achievements, it might become too literal—telling a user "No Match" because they don't have a specific keyword, even though their broader "Profile" (education, titles, industry exposure) suggests they could absolutely do the job.

We can’t let the "Clean Signal" of achievements create a "Blind Spot" for potential.

To fix this, we don't go back to the "Full Profile" mess. Instead, we use the Profile Framing (Positioning Statement) as the safety net.



The "Dual-Signal" Analysis

The AI should perform the analysis using two distinct layers:



The Floor (The Profile Framing): This acts as the "Permission to Play." It tells the AI: "I am a Financial Analyst." Even if the user has 0 achievements saved about "Excel," the AI knows they have that foundational capability because of their role title and years of experience.

The Ceiling (The Achievement Bank): This acts as the "Evidence to Win." It tells the AI: "Here is why I’m better than the other 200 applicants." This is what creates the "80% vs 95%" match score.

How to prevent "False Negatives" (Filtering too hard):

We should implement "Translatable Skill Mapping" in the Analysis phase.



The AI Logic: If a JD asks for "Stakeholder Management" and the user has 0 achievements with that exact phrase, the AI shouldn't just say "Gap."

The Ally Move: The AI should look at the Profile Framing and say: "You haven't added an achievement for 'Stakeholder Management' yet, but as a Senior Analyst, you likely have this experience. Should we draft an achievement for this now?"

The Updated "Analyse" Card UI

To solve your fear of filtering out "gettable" jobs, the Analysis result shouldn't be a hard "Yes/No." It should be a "Distance to Match" indicator.



Direct Match: "Your achievements prove you can do 70% of this role right now."

Bridgeable Gap: "Based on your role as an Analyst, you likely have the other 30%—we just need to frame your 'Reporting' as 'Strategic Communication'."

Hard Gap: "This role requires a CPA. You don't have this in your achievements or profile." (The only time it truly "filters").

Why this is better:

It moves the tool from being a Filter (which feels like another rejection) to being a Coach (which tells them how to bridge the gap). It tells the user: "You CAN get this job, but your current achievements aren't showing it yet. Let's fix that."

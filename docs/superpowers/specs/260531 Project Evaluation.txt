  What's working ✅

  - Cover letters are well-written, on-tone, and genuinely tailored to each JD.
  - Company Insight is the standout new feature — in Job 3 (TravelManagers) it drove the whole opening  paragraph and the salutation. That's exactly what it's for.
  - The "Include in resume" achievements reliably make it into the resume.
  - Small intelligence wins: it caught the "pineapple" instruction (Job 4) and wove in
  work-rights/citizenship (Job 1). The National Police Check was correctly flagged as "not on profile"  rather than faked.

  What's broken ❌  (in priority order)

  1. Bridged gaps don't reach the documents — this is the big one.
  I confirmed it in code: when you generate, the resume request sends only the job description, and   
  the cover letter adds only company/title/tone + Company Insight. The list of gaps you tick is local 
  screen state that is never sent anywhere. So ticking a gap changes a checkbox and nothing else.     
  Worse, your best material dies here — the manually edited bridges with hard numbers ("coordinated 5 
  marketers → 21% productivity", "150% output increase", "21% client satisfaction") never appear in   
  any output. Those are gold and we drop them.

  2. The cover letter contradicts a bridged gap (Job 4). You bridged "I utilised Adobe Creative Suite…  increasing output by 150%", but the letter says "Although I have not had direct experience with     
  Adobe Creative Suite…". Because the bridge never reached the generator, the letter actively undercut  the claim you made. That's not just a miss, it's harmful.

  3. The resume barely changes between jobs. The Skills section is identical in all four (Microsoft   
  Office, Excel, Data Analysis, AI workflows, HubSpot-Meta) — even when the JD screams Adobe Creative 
  Suite, Mailchimp, Cvent, Sugar CRM. Work-experience bullets are light rewordings of the same three  
  achievements. The resume is anchored to the base profile and isn't absorbing JD keywords or bridged 
  skills.

  4. The analysis isn't deterministic. Job 1 run twice (after your restart) gave 60% then 40%, with   
  completely different gaps each time. Your own note — "we are kinda just generating random gaps" — is  correct and well-founded. Re-running the same job shouldn't reshuffle the diagnosis.

  5. Company Insight is inconsistent:
  - Job 2: header rendered but empty (the JSON-truncation bug we just fixed — verify after the staging  deploy).
  - Job 3: citation markers leaked into the text (…growth in the travel sector.[5][6][7]) — also      
  addressed by the fix we just pushed (citation stripping).
  - Salutation: used in Job 3 but the whole verbose string became the greeting — "Dear Head of        
  Marketing or Marketing Manager (National Partnership Office, Surry Hills)," — clunky. And Job 4     
  ignored the suggested contact entirely → generic "Dear Hiring Manager".

  6. Generation glitches. Broken sentences slipped through: "I am a who is solutions-focused" (Job 2),  "bring my skills and experience to a new challenge" (Job 4, missing subject). And a [VERIFY: number 
  of] token surfaced in Job 1's letter even though "150+ assets" was right there in the data — so it  
  asked you to verify a number we already had.

  What we need to do next (priority order)

  1. Wire bridged gaps into the generation payload for both resume and cover letter — especially the  
  manually-edited ones with metrics. This is the highest-leverage fix and unblocks the whole "bridge  
  your gaps" UX. (Ticked gaps currently aren't even persisted — they need to be captured and passed.) 
  2. Add a contradiction guard so the letter never denies experience the candidate just claimed (the  
  Adobe case).
  3. Make the resume actually tailor: inject JD tools/skills into the Skills section and turn bridged 
  statements into real bullets.
  4. Stabilise the analysis (lower temperature / cache the result per job) so re-runs don't change the  %/gaps.
  5. Confirm Company Insight reliability post-deploy (Job 2 empty + Job 3 citations should both be    
  fixed already), and clean up the salutation (use just the role title; always use it when present).  
  6. Tidy generation glitches and stop [VERIFY:] firing when the data exists.

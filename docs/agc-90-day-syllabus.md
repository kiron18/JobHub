# AGC 90-Day Placement Program: Master Syllabus (v2)

**Promise:** Your first tailored application is in the market within 24 hours of joining. Within your first month you are running a complete professional job search: applying daily with quality, reaching decision makers directly, visible on LinkedIn, and interview-ready. Support continues until you are placed.

**Audience:** International graduates in Australia, any field. The spine is universal; personalisation comes from the diagnostic, the Venn exercise, and per-track templates.

**Format:** Five self-paced modules on Skool (video + checklist + tool). Each module teaches a concept, hands over a tool, and ends in a gate: a concrete output posted publicly. Live calls support the modules; they never replace them. A motivated student can finish all five modules in 2 weeks; the recommended pace is 4 weeks. The modules end, the execution never does.

---

## Design principles

1. **Resume out in 24 hours.** The resume is the backbone. The AGC workspace makes a quality first application achievable on day one, so the program demands it.
2. **Concept, then tool, then execution.** Every module follows the same shape: understand why it works (video), get the tool that does the heavy lifting (workspace, templates, checklists), execute and post proof.
3. **Gates, not timelines.** Nothing unlocks by waiting. Each gate is a required post in the Gate Submissions thread: public accountability, and your one-glance view of every student.
4. **Answer once, help everyone.** No DM support. Questions go to the community or the pre-call question thread. AI clusters and preps them; every student hears their name answered on calls.
5. **Coach hours are capped** at ~5 per week regardless of cohort size (Section 8).

## Module 1: Launch. Your resume in the market in 24 hours

**The concept (Lessons 1.1 and 1.2, videos):**

- **1.1 What a resume and cover letter actually are.** Not a life history: a sales document read in seconds. The core lesson: **outcomes achieved, not tasks done.** "Responsible for water quality testing" loses to "Held water quality in target range across commercial systems." This is the single biggest reason qualified people don't get interviews, and it is the standard every document they send must meet. The cover letter's job: connect three of your outcomes to their problem, nothing more.
- **1.2 The Venn: choosing your target.** Three circles: what you **want**, what you have genuine **interest** in, what you have **tangible evidence** for. Your target role sits where they overlap. The honesty rule: if you have no tangible evidence for the role you want, you cannot target it yet, and no resume trick fixes that. You target the nearest role where you do have evidence, and you build evidence toward the role you want (Module 3). Worked example: a systems engineer wanting ML/data/AI roles inventories every project, finds real evidence weighted toward one lane, and leads with that lane.

**The tool (Lesson 1.3, screencast + one-page quickstart PDF):**

The AussieGradCareers workspace, end to end:
1. Upload your resume once. The system builds your diagnostic and your **Profile Bank**.
2. **Fix your Profile Bank first.** It is the source of truth for everything generated. Check every role, date, and achievement; add what is missing (the uni project that never made your resume is often the strongest evidence you have).
3. Set your target role and city. This drives your job feed.
4. Pick a job from the feed, or paste any job you found yourself.
5. Generate the tailored resume and cover letter (and selection criteria for government and university roles).
6. **The 60-second quality check, every time.** Facts true, dates right, summary sounds like you, every bullet an outcome. You are the final gate; never send anything unreviewed.
7. Apply, and the tracker logs it and schedules your follow-up reminders.
8. Set your application goal: daily if your week is even, weekly if you do shift work. Default 10 to 15 tailored applications per week.

**Execution (the 24-hour checklist):** join, upload, correct the bank, run the Venn, generate for 1 to 3 real jobs, quality-check, submit.

**Gate 1:** First application submitted within 48 hours (24 is the challenge, 48 the deadline), posted to Gate Submissions with the generated resume attached. The coach spot-checks early submissions on the next call; the first resume each student produces gets eyes on it.

## Module 2: Outreach. Stop being an anonymous applicant

**The concept (video):** why applications alone fail (the visibility math), and why outreach works here: Australians expect and respect a brief, direct ask. Two motions, taught as one skill:
- **Applied outreach:** every application you care about gets one message to the hiring manager or a team member.
- **Relationship outreach:** conversations with people in your field with no job attached, which is where referrals actually come from.

**The tools:**
- The **template library**: LinkedIn connection requests, post-application notes, email versions, informational chat asks, and the follow-up cadence (the tracker reminds you when).
- **Finding the person:** Hunter.io, Apollo.io, RocketReach free-tier workflow for hiring manager emails.
- **The phone:** the Pre-Interview Beat Sheet, calling about roles, voicemail scripts. Practised live on a call, because this is the scariest one.

**Gate 2:** 10 outreach touches sent and logged, one reply screenshot (or the best attempt) posted.

## Module 3: Content and proof. Become findable

**The concept (video):** **Signal Modeling.** Find five people in your field one to three steps ahead of you who get real engagement. Study their formats and topics. Adapt those proven structures to your own evidence and voice. You are not copying content; you are adopting formats the market has already validated, the way musicians learn standards before writing originals.

**The tools:** LinkedIn profile checklist (headline, about, featured, experience aligned to your Profile Bank), post format templates, a swipe file per track.

**Proof of Work (second half of the module):** the strongest content is something you built. One small, role-relevant project, scoped to ship in one to two weeks: a case study, dashboard, small app, or teardown of a company in your target industry. It closes the Venn gap from Module 1 (tangible evidence toward the role you actually want), feeds your posts, goes on the resume, and gives outreach a reason ("I built X for exactly this problem").

**Gate 3:** Profile passes the checklist, first post published, project declared with a ship date (project ships within the execution loop).

## Module 4: Interview conversion

*(Any student who lands an interview at any point gets immediate access to this module. Interviews outrank the sequence.)*

**The concept (video):** interviews are not exams, they are evidence transfers. **CAR, not STAR:** Context, Action, Result. Tighter, more natural, no forced "task" padding.

**The tools and drills:**
- **CAR story bank:** 8 to 10 stories from the Profile Bank covering the universal competencies (teamwork, conflict, initiative, failure, learning speed, delivery under pressure).
- **The research routine** per interview: company, role, likely questions, your closing questions.
- **Phone screens:** the Beat Sheet applied, because recruiters call unannounced.
- **Every conversation is an interview:** the networking chat, the phone call, the meetup. Your positioned 30-second answer to "so what do you do?"
- **Mock interviews** on the Clinic call, scored against a rubric.
- **Offers:** reading one, grad-market salary reality, when to negotiate and when to sign.

**Gate 4:** CAR bank written, one mock passed live or by recording.

## Module 5: Communication and presence

The module behind every other module: how you come across, in the room and out of it. Social and professional.

**Lessons (short videos, heavy on demonstration):**
- **5.1 First impressions:** walking into a room, the first thing you say, handshake, names.
- **5.2 Body language and dress:** what to wear per context (interview, coffee chat, career fair), posture, eye contact on video calls.
- **5.3 Speaking to be understood:** pace (slow down 20 percent), structure beats accent, answering the actual question first, cutting hedges ("I just", "sort of", "I think maybe").
- **5.4 Commanding a room without arrogance:** self-promotion as data, not bragging. Directness as respect, deference miscalibrated for Australia. Small talk as a protocol you can learn.
- **5.5 The operating system:** rejection math (silence is a funnel stat, not a verdict), the comparison trap, family pressure timelines. Delivered with names students can use on calls ("that's rejection math").

**Gate 5:** One recorded 60-second self-introduction posted, scored against the checklist by a peer and spot-checked by coach.

## The Execution Loop (runs from day 2 until placed)

Modules are consumed once; this runs forever.

**Daily non-negotiables (installed at Gate 1, 45 to 90 minutes or the weekly equivalent):**
1. Hit today's application number through the workspace.
2. Clear tracker follow-up reminders.
3. One outreach touch (from Module 2 onward).
4. Log everything.

**Weekly:** Friday self-review against the goal, numbers posted in the accountability thread. One post per week (from Module 3 onward).

**The iteration rules (taught as a short lesson, used on Clinic calls):**
- 30+ applications, no responses: positioning or resume problem. Revisit the Venn and the bank.
- Responses but no interviews: application quality or role-fit problem.
- Interviews but no offers: conversion problem. Drill Module 4.

Rejection becomes diagnostics, not despair.

## Weekly cadence and coach time budget

Two live group calls per week, third touchpoint async. Total coach load roughly 5 hours per week, flat, at any cohort size.

| Slot | Format | Length |
|---|---|---|
| Tue: **Momentum Call** | Wins, module Q&A, 2 to 3 hot seats (live resume, outreach, or Venn reviews) | 60 to 75 min |
| Fri: **Clinic** | Pre-submitted questions (AI-clustered, prepped notes), pipeline reviews, mock interviews | 60 to 75 min |
| Mon / Wed / Sat | Async batch: coach answers community questions in one sitting per window | 20 to 30 min each |

**Question pipeline:** students post to the pinned Call Questions thread by the night before; Claude clusters by theme, drafts answer notes, flags anyone silent for 2 weeks. Every submitter hears their name on the call. Personal attention, scaled.

**Burnout rules (in the program agreement):** no 1:1s except the onboarding diagnostic call (1:1 time is a paid add-on or an interview-tomorrow emergency); no DM support; recordings posted same day; videos shot in half-day batches.

## Platform: Skool

Community + gated classroom + calendar in one place, and the workspace already has a Skool join flow built in. Levels map to modules; since Skool unlocks by engagement points rather than gate completion, gates are enforced socially through the required Gate Submissions post. Long reference assets (template libraries, the quickstart PDF) attach to lessons.

## Metrics per student (one-glance dashboard)

From the tracker plus gate threads, weekly: applications vs goal, outreach touches, response and interview counts, current module and days stuck, last community activity.

**Red flags triggering a nudge (one message or a call mention, never a 1:1):** 10+ days stuck on a gate, zero applications in a week after Gate 1, silent for 2 weeks, 30+ applications with zero responses.

## Migrating current 1:1 students

Map by output, not session count: application in market through the workspace is Gate 1 passed; outreach running is Gate 2; and so on. Position it straight: "The program is becoming more structured so you get more support, not less: two live calls a week plus a community, instead of one session." Remaining 1:1 sessions become their bridge into the cohort rhythm.

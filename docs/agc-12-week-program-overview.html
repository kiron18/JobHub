The 12-Week Job Landing Program

A structured system that takes a job-ready candidate to a placed one: fully geared in the first two weeks, applying at volume from week two, and interview-ready when the calls come.
How It Is Delivered

On-Demand Module Library

Video lessons paired with one-page cheat sheets. Progression is gated: the next module unlocks when the actions are done, not when the videos are watched. Candidates build up step by step instead of drowning in everything at once.

Two Live Group Sessions Weekly

Working sessions, not lectures. Live resume and application teardowns, real recruiter messages reviewed on screen, interview scenarios and group feedback. Teaching lives on video so live time goes to real problems.

Tracked Application System

Every candidate's activity is visible on my backend: applications sent, goals met, where they are falling short. The minimum standard is 5 applications per day, checked every week. Nobody drifts.

Each module ships with a video lesson and a one-page cheat sheet. Candidates move at their own pace, but the gates keep the pace honest.
The 12-Week Arc
Weeks 1–2 · Fully Geared
Resume, LinkedIn and Seek profiles live, recruiter registrations done, outreach running. First applications go out in week one.
Weeks 1–6 · Structured Sessions
The first six weeks of live sessions follow a set sequence: positioning, resume teardowns, outreach, government selection criteria, and interview foundations.
Weeks 3–12 · Apply & Land
Volume applications at 5 per day. Live sessions turn responsive: mock interviews, real scenarios as they happen, offers and negotiation.
Every week, on top of the sessions: an accountability check against each candidate's numbers, straight from the tracking backend. Applications sent, responses received, interviews booked, and an honest conversation about anything falling short.
Why it is built this way. Teaching is one-way, so it belongs on video where candidates can move at their own pace. Live time is reserved for what a video can never do: real problems, real messages, real interviews, in front of a group facing the same challenges. The group setting is deliberate. Candidates see others pushing through the same struggles, get proof that placement is possible, and stay accountable to more than just me.

## Tier 1: Dream Companies

**Target: 10 companies.**

These are the ones where you think, honestly, "I would work for these people for free." Do not actually work for free. But that gut feeling is the signal we are looking for.

A Tier 1 company meets two tests at once:

1. **Your experience is highly relevant to them.** They run enterprise SaaS accounts that need retaining and growing, which is exactly what you do. You would add value in your first month.
2. **You are obsessive about them.** You care about what they build, how they build it, and why they exist. You would happily use their product, and you would have an opinion about it without being asked.

That second test is what most people skip, and it is the one that gets you hired. When you are genuinely drawn to a company, it shows in every email, every conversation, every line of your application. You cannot fake it and you do not have to.

**The NSW filter, and why it helps you.** Every company on this list sits in NSW: Sydney, Newcastle, or regional NSW that your 190 allows. This is not a constraint to apologise for. It is leverage. A company hiring you takes on zero sponsorship risk and zero relocation risk. You are already here, already on a permanent pathway, already settled in the state. To a hiring manager weighing two similar candidates, that is one less thing that can go wrong. We will say it out loud in your applications.

**Where to aim, and why it fits you.** Sydney has a deep cluster of SaaS companies with real account management and customer success teams, from the marquee names down to the mid-market firms where one good account manager genuinely shapes the company. The smartest place for you to start is the ones where your finance and FSI background is a bonus, because that is where you beat a generalist instead of competing with one.

Your finished Tier 1, the specific ten companies matched to your exact strengths, the person to reach inside each one, and the warm angle in, is the first thing we build together. This framework is the map. The named list is the route, and it is tuned to you, not handed out cold.

## Tier 2: Strong Fit

**Target: 15 to 20 companies.**

This is where your real edge lives, and almost nobody plays here properly.

Tier 1 is the obvious SaaS names. Everyone applies there. Tier 2 is everywhere else: the companies where your account growth and retention skills create enormous value, but who would never show up if you only searched job boards for "account manager."

Here is the insight to hold onto. Plenty of NSW companies have a sales team that is good at winning new logos and almost nothing built to keep and grow the customers they already have. They feel the churn, they sense they are leaving money on the table at renewal, and they have no one whose actual job is retention and expansion. That gap is your entire opportunity, because that is precisely what you have done.

So Tier 2 is built by thinking beyond the obvious SaaS brands:

- **Fintech, regtech and wealthtech** across Sydney. Your Deloitte FSI years and your audit and compliance fluency make you unusually credible here. Many are growing fast and under-resourced on account management.
- **ESG, sustainability and impact-tech** companies. This is your differentiated niche from Goodera and Dasra, and it is a field that is expanding in NSW. Few candidates can speak both account growth and ESG.
- **Professional services and consulting firms** that sell ongoing engagements and live or die on renewals and client relationships.
- **Insurance, superannuation and financial-services providers** with large client bases and thin relationship-management functions.
- **Mid-market software and services companies** that have grown past the founder-led-sales stage and now urgently need someone to own retention.

You will not find most of these by searching for your job title, because they often do not advertise the role until someone shows them they need it. You find them by deciding which one or two of these worlds you can speak to, then going after the companies in them directly through LinkedIn. That is exactly what the LinkedIn Playbook is for.

This tier is where someone with your experience and a bit of courage gets hired into a role that grows around them.

## Tier 3: Momentum

**Target: as many as you can sensibly handle.**

Tier 1 and Tier 2 are where you want to end up. Tier 3 is what keeps you sharp, employed, and confident while you get there.

These are NSW companies where you are clearly, comfortably qualified. The account management or customer success role exists, it is posted, and you could do it well without stretching. They might not make you obsessive, and that is fine. Their job is different:

- **They give you interview reps.** Interviewing is a skill that rusts. You want to be warm and practised by the time a Tier 1 interview lands, not cold.
- **They protect your income and your pathway.** A steady role in your field keeps your 190 situation stable and takes the desperation out of your search. Desperation is the thing recruiters smell, and the thing that makes you accept the wrong offer.
- **They build your reference bank.** Recent NSW managers who will vouch for you are worth more than any line on a resume.

Do not pour your soul into Tier 3 the way you do Tier 1. Use clean, slightly tailored applications and keep the volume reasonable. Tier 3 is the floor that lets you reach for the ceiling.

## How to actually run this

1. **The lane is locked.** Account management and customer success. Everything below points there.
2. **Build Tier 1 to exactly ten.** Quality over quantity. Research each until you could hold a real conversation about them.
3. **Build Tier 2 to fifteen or twenty.** Pick one or two of your edges, fintech, ESG, or financial services, and go deep.
4. **Keep Tier 3 flowing in the background.** Reps, income, references.
5. **Attack Tier 1 and Tier 2 through LinkedIn, not job boards.** See the playbook. This is the part that separates you from everyone still sorting by date.

# Your LinkedIn Playbook

### How to attack LinkedIn, built for Anurag

---

## Start here: why this is the whole game

Around seventy percent of roles in Australia are filled through networking, not job applications. Read that again. For every ten jobs that get filled, seven of them go to someone who had a relationship, a referral, or a conversation before the role was even decided. Only three go through the front door of a job board.

Most people spend almost all of their energy on those three. They polish applications and refresh Seek and wonder why it is so slow. Meanwhile the seven are being filled in conversations they were never part of.

This playbook is how you get into those conversations. LinkedIn is not a job board. It is the room where the seventy percent happens. We are going to treat it that way.

One rule sits above everything else here: **relationship first, ask last.** Nobody owes you a job. But people will genuinely help someone they have had a warm, low-pressure conversation with. Relationship building is literally your profession, so this is home turf for you. The entire system below is built on it.

## Step 1: Turn your profile into a landing page

Before you message a single person, your profile has to do its job. When someone you have reached out to clicks your name, they decide in seconds whether you are worth a reply. Make the decision easy.

**Your headline.** This is the most valuable line you own. It shows up everywhere, in every search and every message. Do not waste it on "Open to work" or your current title alone. State the one role you are aiming at and the value you bring.

A weak headline: *Business Development Manager*

A strong headline: *Enterprise Account Manager and Customer Success | I retain and grow SaaS revenue through relationships that last | Sydney, NSW*

Notice it names the role, names the value, and grounds you in NSW. That last part matters more than people think, which is the next point.

**Your location and your NSW signal.** Set your location to Sydney, New South Wales. When recruiters and hiring managers filter, they filter by location, and being clearly in NSW puts you in the right pile every time. Your 190 pathway is a strength here. You are already settled, already on a permanent track, no sponsorship needed. Where it fits naturally, say it. "Permanent resident pathway, based in NSW, no sponsorship required" removes a worry from a hiring manager's mind before they even ask.

**Your About section.** This is your story in your own voice, not a list of skills. Three short paragraphs:

1. Who you are and the one thing you are great at. For you, that is keeping enterprise clients and growing them.
2. Proof. The kind of accounts you have retained and expanded, and what changed because you did. Lead with USD 800K retained, around 10% growth, and the 75% NPS turnaround.
3. What you are looking for now and an open invitation to connect.

Write it like a human talking, not a resume. The same calm, specific voice you would use with a client.

**Your Featured section.** Pin two or three things that show your value. A short case study of an account you turned around, a one-page summary of your approach to retention, or a post you have written about customer success. Proof beats claims every time.

**Turn on Open to Work, quietly.** Use the setting that signals to recruiters only, not the green banner across your photo, unless you are fully public in your search. Specify account management and customer success, and Sydney and NSW as your locations.

## Step 2: Build your target list inside LinkedIn

You already have your three tiers from the Target Company Framework. Now you find the actual humans inside them.

For each Tier 1 and Tier 2 company, you are looking for two kinds of people:

1. **The people who would be your manager or your peers.** Heads of Customer Success, Account Management leads, VPs of Customer Success, Sales Directors, and at smaller companies the founders themselves. These are the people who feel the retention problem you solve.
2. **The people who do the hiring.** Internal recruiters and talent partners at the larger companies.

Use LinkedIn search. Type the company name, filter to people, and search titles like "head of customer success", "account management", "customer success manager", "revenue", "talent acquisition". Build a simple list. A spreadsheet with company, name, role, and a column for where you are up to with them is more than enough.

Aim for three to five real people per Tier 1 company. You do not need hundreds of contacts. You need the right twenty or thirty conversations.

## Step 3: Reach out, relationship first

This is where most people get it wrong. They send a connection request and immediately ask for a job, or worse, send nothing and just hope. Both fail. You already know better than most, because this is exactly how you grow an account.

The whole game is a three-touch sequence: open a door with no ask, give something of value before you take, and only then make a request so small a busy person can say yes in thirty seconds. Done right, a cold contact becomes a warm chat, and a warm chat becomes "actually, we are hiring, you should apply."

The exact wording is where this lives or dies, and getting it wrong burns the contact for good. So the word-for-word message templates for each of the three touches, written in your voice and tuned for account and customer success leaders, are part of the kit we build together. ## Step 4: Become visible, so people come to you

Outreach is you going to people. Content is people coming to you. Do both.

You do not need to be an influencer. You need to be visible to the few hundred people who matter for your search, so that when a Tier 1 target looks at your profile after you connect, they see someone who clearly understands retention and relationships. That quietly closes the deal. Your rare double background in financial services and ESG is the angle almost nobody else can play.

The specific content plan, what to post, how often, and the angles that position you as the obvious hire, is something we build to your strengths once we are set up.

Consistency beats intensity, and the job search punishes you for stopping exactly when stopping feels easiest. The difference between people who land roles through LinkedIn and people who do not is almost never talent. It is having a simple, repeatable system and running it daily without burning out.

## The Australian details that quietly matter

A few cultural norms that international candidates often miss, and that move the needle here more than people expect:

- **After any call or interview, send a thank-you within twenty-four hours.** In Australia this is expected, and recruiters notice when it is missing. It is one of the lowest-effort, highest-signal moves you can make.
- **After you apply or have a chat, a short, polite follow-up a week later is normal and welcome.** Silence is not patience, it is being forgotten. A warm nudge keeps you alive in their mind.
- **Tone is everything here. Warm, direct, no hard sell, no desperation.** The calm, specific person wins. The pushy one gets ignored. You already have the right instincts for this, it is what you do with clients.

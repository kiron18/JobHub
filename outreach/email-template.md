# Outreach email for international student societies

Send this to the committee of a university's international student society (president, events officer, or careers/welfare officer). Replace every bracketed token. Keep it short. The whole pitch is value to their members, not to you.

---

## Subject line (pick one)

- A free job-hunt workshop for your members (no cost, no catch)
- Helping your international students land their first Australian job
- Free workshop offer for [Society name]

---

## Email body

Hi [First name / Committee],

I run JobHub, a tool that helps international students and graduates land their first job in Australia. Before that, I was one of them, figuring out the local hiring rules the hard way.

I would love to give your members a free 20-minute workshop: **how to actually land your first job in Australia.** It is practical, not a sales pitch. By the end they will know:

- The three local hiring rules that internationals are never told
- What a recruiter looks for in the first six seconds of a resume
- How to find the hidden job market, including visa-sponsoring employers

Everyone who attends also gets a free resume diagnostic, an honest review of their resume against live Australian job ads. It is the kind of review career coaches here charge $300 to $500 for, and it is my gift to your members.

I can fit into a regular meeting or run it as a standalone event, in person or online, whatever suits you. Could we find 20 minutes in the next few weeks?

Happy to send the slides ahead of time so you can see exactly what your members would get.

Thanks for the work you do for international students. It matters more than most people realise.

Warm regards,
[Your name]
JobHub
[your-email] · [yourwebsite.com]

---

## A few notes for you (not part of the email)

- **Keep it under 200 words.** Committees are volunteers reading on their phones. The version above is close to that.
- **Lead with the gift, not the brand.** The first thing they should feel is "free value for our members," not "someone wants to sell to us."
- **Personalise one line if you can.** A single specific reference (an event they ran, their faculty mix) lifts reply rates more than anything else. Drop it into the first paragraph.
- **Send from a real human address**, not a no-reply or generic info@. Societies trust people, not brands.
- **Follow up once, after 5 to 7 days.** One short, friendly nudge. Then leave it.
- **Offer to send the deck.** Letting them preview `society-deck.html` removes the risk for them and proves you are not going to pitch their members.
